## Executive Summary

This document defines the comprehensive solution scope for an EPLMS platform that addresses the complete policy lifecycle from authoring through continuous adherence. Based on our domain assessment and global standards analysis, organizations struggle across multiple stages, with the most acute pain points in **Stages 2-3 (Authoring & Approval)** and **Stages 5-7 (Acknowledgment, Compliance Monitoring, Review/Revision)**.

**Core Strategic Positioning:**
*"We provide an end-to-end platform for policy lifecycle management - from collaborative authoring and structured approval workflows, through contextual distribution and comprehension-verified acknowledgment, to AI-powered retrieval and continuous compliance monitoring."*

**What We Own:** Policy Authoring, Approval Workflows, Distribution, Adherence, Engagement, and Continuous Compliance (Stages 2-7)
**What We Don't Own:** Initial policy need identification (Stage 1) and long-term archival systems (Stage 8)

## 4. Policy Authoring & Approval Workflows (Stages 2-3)

### 4.1 Problem Definition: The Current Reality

**Domain Assessment Findings:**
- **Stage 2 (Authoring):** Major Gap - No version control, collaboration chaos, no audit trail (Section 2.1.3)
- **Stage 3 (Review/Approval):** Major Gap - Email-based, informal approvals, no enforcement (Section 2.1.3)
- **Customer Challenges:**
  - 68% cite "time-consuming manual processes" as major challenge
  - 71% cite "version control and document currency" as major challenge
  - Average policy takes 8-12 weeks from draft to approval (Domain Assessment, Section 2.1)

**Observed Reality (How It Actually Happens):**

**Authoring Process:**
- Author creates Word document, often by copying/pasting from templates found via Google
- Multi-author collaboration via email attachments creates version chaos ("WFH_Policy_v2_final_FINAL.docx")
- Multiple copies exist simultaneously in personal drives, email, shared drives - no single source of truth
- Section ownership is verbal ("You do Section 3") with no enforcement - people edit outside their sections
- Manual merging of edits from multiple contributors takes 2-3 hours per iteration
- No audit trail of who changed what when

**Approval Process:**
- Review and approval conducted entirely via email threads
- Approval is informal ("Looks good" email reply or verbal in meeting)
- No consolidated approval record - scattered across emails, meeting notes
- No enforcement of approval sequence (can skip required approvers)
- Hard to prove "who approved this" 6 months later for audit
- Bottlenecks invisible (policy stuck with one reviewer for weeks, nobody knows)

**System of Record:**
- During drafting: No system of record - multiple copies everywhere
- After approval: Policy uploaded to SharePoint/Confluence/Google Drive
- But draft versions persist in drives/emails (zombie files)
- No metadata captured systematically (owner, approval date, review date, version history)

**Standards Violation:**
- **ISO 9001:** Requires "review and approval for suitability" - current process is informal, unauditable
- **21 CFR Part 11:** Requires "authority checks" and "time-stamped audit trails" - email doesn't provide this
- **SOC 2:** Requires "documented approval process" - scattered emails don't qualify

#### **3. Digital Signatures & Formal Approvals**
