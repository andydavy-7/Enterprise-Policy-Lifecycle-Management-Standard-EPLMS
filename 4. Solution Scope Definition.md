# Enterprise Policy Lifecycle Management (EPLMS)
## Solution Scope Definition

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** December 2025
**Purpose:** Define the solution scope for policy adherence and engagement platform, validated against global standards and market assessment

---

## Executive Summary

This document defines the solution scope for an EPLMS platform focused on **policy adherence and engagement** - the critical gap in the current market. Based on our domain assessment and global standards analysis, we have identified that while organizations struggle across the entire policy lifecycle, **Stages 5-7 (Acknowledgment, Compliance Monitoring, Review/Revision)** represent the most acute pain points and the greatest opportunity for differentiation.

**Core Strategic Positioning:**
*"We are the engagement layer for policy compliance - ensuring employees don't just acknowledge policies, but actually use them in daily work."*

**What We Own:** Policy Adherence & Engagement (Initial adoption through continuous usage)
**What We Don't Own (Initially):** Policy Authoring & Approval Workflows (System of record capabilities)

### 1.2 Alignment with International Standards Requirements

Our scope directly addresses mandatory requirements from global compliance frameworks:

#### **ISO 9001:2015 - Section 7.5 (Documented Information)**

**Standard Requirement:**
> "Documented information must be controlled to ensure it is **available and suitable for use**, where and when it is needed, and **adequately protected**."

**Our Scope Addresses:**
- ‚úÖ **Availability:** AI-powered retrieval, multi-channel access (Slack/Teams/mobile)
- ‚úÖ **Suitability:** Multi-modal delivery (video, infographic, audio), comprehension verification
- ‚úÖ **Access control:** Role-based policy distribution, integration with existing systems

**Standard Requirement:**
> "Control of distribution, access, retrieval, and use"

**Our Scope Addresses:**
- ‚úÖ **Distribution:** Multi-channel notification (email, Slack, Teams, SMS)
- ‚úÖ **Retrieval:** AI search, chatbot interface, 5-second policy answers
- ‚úÖ **Use tracking:** Analytics on who accessed, searched, acknowledged

#### **ISO 27001:2013 - Control A.5.1 (Information Security Policies)**

**Standard Requirement:**
> "Policies must be **communicated to employees** and relevant external parties"

**Our Scope Addresses:**
- ‚úÖ **Communication:** Contextual delivery, in-workflow surfacing, continuous reinforcement

**Our Scope Addresses:**
- ‚úÖ **Acknowledgment tracking:** Digital attestation with timestamps, individual attribution

**Our Scope Addresses:**

## 3. Solution Scope Definition

### 3.1 IN SCOPE: Policy Adherence & Engagement Platform

Our solution owns **three integrated dimensions** that together solve the adherence problem:

---

#### **Dimension 1: Policy Adoption**
*"Ensuring employees acknowledge AND understand policies"*

**Problem Addressed:**
- Current state: Policies emailed, 30-50% acknowledgment, no verification of understanding (Domain Assessment, Section 3.1)
- Standard requirement: ISO 27001 requires "evidence of acknowledgment and understanding"
- Gap: 78% of organizations cite acknowledgment as major challenge

**Core Capabilities:**

1. **Contextual Delivery**
   - Multi-channel distribution (email, Slack, Teams, SMS, mobile push)
   - Role-based policy assignment (e.g., managers get Manager Policies, CA employees get CA-specific)
   - Just-in-time surfacing (e.g., employee requests WFH ‚Üí WFH policy snippet surfaces)
   - **Outcome:** 95%+ notification reach within 24 hours (vs. 40% email open rate)

2. **Comprehension Verification**
   - Not passive "I acknowledge" checkbox
   - Adaptive micro-learning: 2-3 questions to verify understanding
   - AI re-explains if wrong, re-quizzes
   - Only marks "acknowledged" when comprehension demonstrated
   - **Outcome:** 90%+ comprehension pass rate, proof of understanding for auditors

3. **Multi-Modal Delivery**
   - AI-generated video summaries (90 seconds)
   - Interactive Q&A chatbot
   - Infographics for visual learners
   - Audio for accessibility/frontline workers
   - **Outcome:** Employees choose format, higher engagement (25-35% increase per Domain Assessment, Section 2.2)

4. **Friction-Free UX**
   - Mobile-first: Acknowledge on phone in 60 seconds
   - In-app: Native Slack/Teams (no forced portal login)
   - Gamification: Leaderboards, badges, completion streaks
   - Social proof: "92% of your team has acknowledged"
   - **Outcome:** 85-95% acknowledgment completion (vs. 30-50% manual, Domain Assessment Section 2.1)

5. **Engagement Mechanics**
   - Automated reminders (3-day, 1-day, same-day)
   - Manager escalation (when direct reports don't acknowledge)
   - Deadline enforcement (optional: block access until acknowledged)
   - Progress tracking (employee sees "3 of 5 policies done")
   - **Outcome:** 90%+ completion within 30 days (guaranteed)

**Standards Alignment:**
- ‚úÖ ISO 27001: Evidence of acknowledgment and understanding
- ‚úÖ SOC 2: Communication to affected parties, attestation records
- ‚úÖ 21 CFR Part 11: Individual accountability, timestamped records

---

#### **Dimension 2: Policy Compliance Co-Pilot**
*"Helping employees understand and apply policies in daily work"*

**Problem Addressed:**
- Current state: Policies acknowledged once, forgotten within weeks; no ongoing usage (Domain Assessment, Section 3.1)
- Standard requirement: ISO 9001 requires documented information "available and suitable for use, where and when needed"
- Gap: Employees can't find policies when needed (53% cite discoverability as challenge)

**Core Capabilities:**

1. **Policy Retrieval & Search**
   - AI-powered semantic search (not keyword matching)
   - Natural language queries: "Can I work from Mexico?" ‚Üí instant answer with policy citation
   - Chatbot interface (Slack/Teams/web/mobile)
   - Voice-enabled (accessibility + frontline workers)
   - **Outcome:** 5-second policy retrieval (vs. 10-min SharePoint search, Domain Assessment Section 3.1)

2. **Policy Assistant (AI-Powered)**
   - Employee asks: "Can I expense this $150 client meal?"
   - AI searches policy corpus, answers in natural language
   - Cites specific policy section and rationale
   - Learns from questions to improve answers
   - **Outcome:** 80% of policy questions answered without HR/Legal involvement

3. **Just-in-Time Guidance**
   - Integrates with workflow tools (Expensify, JIRA, Salesforce, Workday)
   - Proactive nudges: "That expense is over $50 limit without approval - see Expense Policy Section 2.4"
   - Contextual policy surfacing at point of decision
   - **Outcome:** 50% reduction in policy violations (pre-violation intervention)

4. **Continuous Reinforcement**
   - Periodic micro-learning: "Policy pop quiz" (gamified)
   - Scenario-based learning: "What would you do if...?"
   - "Policy of the Day" tips (Slack/email)
   - **Outcome:** Policy recall sustained over 6+ months (vs. degradation with one-time training)

5. **Personal Policy Library**
   - Bookmark frequently-referenced policies
   - Recent searches and views
   - Role-specific "Policies for You" recommendations
   - Subscribe to policy update notifications
   - **Outcome:** Employees build relationship with policies, not one-time transaction

**Standards Alignment:**
- ‚úÖ ISO 9001: Documented information available when needed
- ‚úÖ ISO 27001: Ongoing communication, periodic review
- ‚úÖ SOC 2: Continuous compliance, not point-in-time

---

#### **Dimension 3: Engagement Layer Architecture**
*"Plug into existing systems - don't force migration"*

**Problem Addressed:**
- Current state: Policies scattered across SharePoint, Confluence, Google Drive, intranet (Domain Assessment, Section 3.1)
- Customer pain: Don't want to migrate; already invested in document management systems
- Competitive advantage: We're engagement layer, not system of record

**Core Capabilities:**

1. **Connect to Existing Policy Stores**
   - APIs/connectors to SharePoint, Confluence, Google Drive, Box
   - Index policies across systems (we don't host, we reference)
   - Sync on policy updates (real-time or scheduled)
   - **Outcome:** No migration required, works with what they have

2. **Policy Discovery & Indexing**
   - Auto-discover policies in connected systems
   - Extract text, metadata, structure
   - AI categorization and tagging
   - Identify current vs. outdated versions
   - **Outcome:** Unified view of all policies, regardless of storage location

3. **Trigger-Based Acknowledgment Workflows**
   - When new policy published in SharePoint ‚Üí our platform triggers acknowledgment workflow
   - When policy updated ‚Üí re-acknowledgment for affected users
   - **Outcome:** Automated, no manual intervention needed

4. **Analytics & Feedback Loop**
   - Dashboard for policy owners: acknowledgment rates, time-to-acknowledge, retrieval patterns
   - AI recommendations: "Policy X has low acknowledgment (60% vs. 90% avg) - suggest: shorten, simplify language, better title"
   - Violation correlation: "Expense Policy searched 100x but violations high ‚Üí policy unclear or unrealistic"
   - **Outcome:** Continuous improvement of policies based on usage data

5. **Integration Ecosystem**
   - **Policy Stores:** SharePoint, Confluence, Google Drive, Box
   - **Communication:** Slack, Microsoft Teams, Email (SendGrid), SMS (Twilio)
   - **HRIS:** Workday, BambooHR, ADP (for employee data, role-based assignment)
   - **Workflow Tools:** Expensify, Salesforce, JIRA (for just-in-time guidance)
   - **LMS (optional):** Cornerstone, Docebo (for training integration)
   - **Outcome:** Lives where employees work, not separate portal

**Standards Alignment:**
- ‚úÖ ISO 9001: Control of distribution, access, retrieval
- ‚úÖ SOC 2: Centralized policy management, evidence retention
- ‚úÖ 21 CFR Part 11: Audit trail across systems

### 3.3 What This Scope Does NOT Include (MVP)

To maintain focus and differentiation, the following are **explicitly out of scope** for the initial platform:

#### ‚ùå **Out of Scope (MVP):**

1. **Policy Authoring Platform**
   - Customers use Microsoft Word, Google Docs, specialized tools
   - We ingest policies (PDF/Word upload), don't replace authoring tools
   - **Rationale:** Authoring is commoditized; not our differentiation
   - **Future:** May add templates, AI-assisted drafting as enhancement

2. **Approval Workflow Engine**
   - Customers approve via email, meetings, existing processes
   - We trigger acknowledgment AFTER approval (not during)
   - **Rationale:** Workflow automation is enterprise GRC territory; complex, not MVP-critical
   - **Future:** May add lightweight approval for small teams (see Placeholder Section 4)

3. **System of Record for Policies**
   - Customers store policies in SharePoint, Confluence, Google Drive
   - We index and reference, don't replace storage
   - **Rationale:** Engagement layer positioning; no migration barrier
   - **Future:** We remain engagement layer; never become system of record

4. **Full GRC Suite Capabilities**
   - Risk management, incident management, vendor management
   - Audit orchestration, regulatory change management
   - **Rationale:** Too broad; dilutes focus on adherence
   - **Future:** Integrations with GRC platforms (MetricStream, ServiceNow), not competition

5. **Detailed Compliance Reporting (Beyond Policies)**
   - SOX controls testing, penetration test reports, etc.
   - **Rationale:** Outside policy adherence scope
   - **Future:** We provide policy compliance evidence; GRC platforms handle broader reporting

6. **Policy Content Creation Services**
   - Writing policies for customers (consulting)
   - Legal review, regulatory analysis
   - **Rationale:** We're a software platform, not a service
   - **Future:** May partner with consultants, provide templates, but not core offering

## 4. Placeholder: Policy Authoring & Approval Workflows

### 4.1 Context

While our core focus is **policy adherence and engagement (Stages 5-7)**, we recognize that **Stages 2-3 (Authoring & Approval)** are also pain points in the market:

**Domain Assessment Findings:**
- **Stage 2 (Authoring):** Major Gap - No version control, collaboration chaos, no audit trail (Section 2.1.3)
- **Stage 3 (Review/Approval):** Major Gap - Email-based, informal approvals, no enforcement (Section 2.1.3)
- **Customer Challenge:** 68% cite "time-consuming manual processes" as major challenge (Section 4.1)

**Global Standard Requirements:**
- **ISO 9001:** Review and approval for suitability and adequacy
- **21 CFR Part 11:** Authority checks, digital signatures
- **SOC 2:** Documented approval process

### 4.2 Strategic Question

**Should we include authoring and approval workflows in our scope?**

**Arguments FOR:**
- Provides end-to-end solution (Stages 2-7)
- Reduces integration complexity (one platform vs. multiple tools)
- Creates more data (drafts, approvals) to improve adherence (e.g., who drafted policy knows it best)
- Differentiates from pure "engagement layer" competitors
- Addresses 68% customer pain point (manual processes)

**Arguments AGAINST:**
- Dilutes focus on our core differentiation (adherence, not authoring)
- Competes directly with enterprise GRC vendors (workflow is their strength)
- Increases MVP complexity and time-to-market
- Customers already have solutions (Word, email, GRC tools) - high switching cost
- "Engagement layer" positioning is cleaner without authoring/approval

### 4.3 Deliberation Required

**To Be Addressed:**
1. **Market Positioning:** Does adding authoring/approval make us a "full platform" or dilute our "engagement layer" story?
2. **Customer Segmentation:** Do SMBs need authoring/approval more than mid-market/enterprise?
3. **MVP Scope:** Can we defer authoring/approval to V2 without losing competitive advantage?
4. **Technical Architecture:** If we add later, does it integrate cleanly or require re-architecture?
5. **Go-to-Market:** Do we sell authoring/approval as separate module or bundled?

**Next Steps:**
- Conduct customer interviews: How painful is authoring/approval vs. adherence?
- Competitive analysis: How well do current workflow tools actually work?
- Technical feasibility: Effort to build authoring/approval vs. adherence modules
- Strategic decision: Include in MVP, defer to V2, or never include
